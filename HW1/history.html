The Empire

The Roman Empire was founded by Octavian Caesar, Augustus, in the year 27 BC. Following the Death of his Uncle Julius Caesar Augustus was thrown into a war with Mark Anthoney and Cleopatra. This war resulted in the death of both Anthoney and Cleopatra, the capture of Egypt for the empire, a major source of food that fed the whole empire and was a major catalyst in its expansion, and instillining Augustus as the Princepts of the Empire meaning “First Citizen”. Augustus was known for acting as the head of state and an elected official while pulling the strings in the back of the Senate. Contrary to popular belief Rome was not always an “empire” it was a Kingdom when the city of Rome was first founded in 743 BC and it became a republic in 509 BC. Even after the Founding of the Empire the Senate was still intact for the rest of the empire’s existence, even if they were just a figurehead under the emperor.This system would continue for almost 400 years. Until the Fall of the Western Empire in 476 under the emperor Romulus Augustus.

Augustus

Emperor Augustus was born in 63 BC, he would struggle with sickness his whole life and would often be considered a coward for always suddenly “falling ill” on the eve of battle. Much of the political infrastructure created by Julius Caesar would be used by Augustus to ease himself and his descendants into power.
